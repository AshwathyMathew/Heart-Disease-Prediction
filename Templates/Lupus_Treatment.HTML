Treatment for Lupus Disease

Medications

These synthetic hormones mimic cortisone and are used to quickly reduce inflammation and pain, but can have significant side effects, so they're often used in conjunction with other medications. Immunosuppressants: These medications suppress the immune system, helping to reduce the severity of lupus symptoms and prevent flares. Examples include cyclophosphamide (Cytoxan) and mycophenolate (Cellcept).

Lifestyle Changes

Healthy Diet:Eating a balanced diet rich in fruits, vegetables, and whole grains can help maintain a healthy weight and reduce inflammation. Regular Exercise: Engaging in physical activity can improve mood, energy levels, and sleep quality, while also helping to manage pain and fatigue.
