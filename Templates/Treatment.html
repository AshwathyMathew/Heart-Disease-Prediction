Treatment for Heart Disease

Lifestyle Changes

Diet: Following a heart-healthy diet low in saturated and trans fats, cholesterol, and sodium. Exercise: Regular physical activity can strengthen the heart and improve overall cardiovascular health. Stress Management: Techniques like yoga, meditation, or deep breathing can help reduce stress, which can contribute to heart disease. Quitting Smoking: Smoking significantly increases the risk of heart disease and should be avoided or stopped.

Medication

Statins: Help lower cholesterol levels. Beta-blockers: Reduce blood pressure and heart rate. ACE inhibitors: Lower blood pressure and help prevent heart failure. Diuretics: Help reduce fluid buildup in the body, which can be a problem in heart failure. Antiplatelet drugs: Prevent blood clots. Anticoagulants: Thins the blood to prevent clots.

Procedures and Surgeries

Angioplasty and Stenting: A procedure to widen blocked arteries in the heart. Coronary Artery Bypass Graft (CABG): A surgery to bypass blocked arteries. Heart Valve Surgery: Repair or replacement of damaged heart valves. Pacemaker: An implanted device to regulate the heart's rhythm. Implantable Cardioverter-Defibrillator (ICD): A device that can deliver electrical shocks to restore a normal heart rhythm. Heart Transplant: Replacing a diseased heart with a healthy donor heart.

Other Treatments

Cardiac Rehabilitation: A supervised exercise program to improve heart health after a heart attack or other heart procedures. Left Ventricular Assist Device (LVAD): A mechanical pump that helps the heart pump blood. Ablation: A procedure to destroy abnormal heart tissue that is causing irregular heart rhythms.
